# Project Overview

## Project Name
LectronicArt Skool HQ

## One-Sentence Mission
Build a creator-first learning community and product ecosystem that helps artists, creators, and one-person entrepreneurs build AI-powered creative machines.

## Why This Project Exists
Creative people want to use AI to make art, video, content, digital products, and simple automated systems, but most education is either too technical, too generic, or aimed at automation agencies rather than creative builders.

## Problem Being Solved
LectronicArt Skool HQ turns scattered ideas, tools, workflows, and experiments into beginner-friendly systems that help creative people make better work, publish consistently, and build income-producing assets.

## Target Audience
- AI-curious artists and visual creators
- AI video makers and content creators
- One-person entrepreneurs and digital-product builders
- Beginners who want practical workflows without becoming full-time programmers
- Creators who want to use AI for art, content, systems, and monetization

## Main Outcomes
- A strong creator-first brand around AI art, video, content systems, digital products, and simple automation
- A Skool community with useful lessons, workflows, templates, challenges, and member outcomes
- A reusable content and product engine that turns learning into proof, proof into content, and content into growth
- A portfolio of digital products, workshops, and optional paid support offers
- A Codex-native beginner learning product, zero2codex, as a major education and trust-building asset

## Current Phase
Active MVP implementation.

The repository now contains the course shell, local progress, complete playable
Levels 1 through 4, and browser-safe foundational, terminal, and Git
simulations.

## Definition of Success
Success means LectronicArt Skool HQ has:
- A clear, differentiated positioning
- A working content-to-community-to-product flywheel
- A visible beginner-friendly learning experience
- Consistent audience proof and member outcomes
- At least one repeatable revenue path that does not depend on custom agency work
- A durable internal operating system that survives new chats and changing plans

## Important Constraints
- Do not position the project as a generic AI automation agency school.
- Prioritize creative builders, artists, video makers, content creators, and solo entrepreneurs.
- Teach practical execution, beginner-friendly workflows, real-world monetization, and strong branding.
- Keep major claims about OpenAI Codex grounded in current official documentation.
- Avoid building a giant product before proving a small, compelling MVP.
- Keep software course simulations browser-safe; do not imply the course can run learner commands or access learner accounts.
